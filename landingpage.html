What do we do?

We aim to develop young Muslim women to discover their potential; spiritually, academically and socially! We run fortnightly sessions where we do different things with the girls ranging from Islamic Tarbya to different activities including sports in every session and fun trips throughout the academic year! Activities and syllabus

Our programme focuses on developing the girlsâ€™ personalities, mind-sets, and their Islamic knowledge. We do this through coving topics that we feel will be the key to unlocking their potential. Our sessions cover the following:

Islam and faith: in these sessions we aim to provide the girls reasons why we are Muslims and why Islam is the truth which helps them reflect.

Salah: we teach the girls the importance of the prayer and how to focus when praying. Also cover topics like the inner dimensions of the prayer and what benefits it brings to our life.

Seerah: in this module we cover the life of the prophet (pbuh) from different angles like how he was as a father, as a husband, as a friend, and as a leader.

Mental health: we cover mental health issues that young people may face and we teach the girls proactive steps to be look after their mental wellbeing.

Self-development sessions: in this we teach the girls how to be productive, how to dream big for the future and we also do different career sessions.

Speakers: throughout the year, we aim to bring different inspirational Speakers like psychologists, doctors, and other career professionls to inspire the girls and answer any questions they may have in their area of expertise.

Mentoring:We aim to match our young people with mentors in their aspiring fields.

Sports: every session a professional instructor runs a PE session with the girls where they do different types of sports. Our aim behind integrating sports into our sessions is to build team skills in our girls and also as the saying goes: "healthy body, healthy mind"

Trips: In order to build an affective bond between our members, we plan day trips at least twice a year. We also undertake practical activities like volunteering. The girls have previously volunteered at soup kitchens, senior peoples homes and organised picnics for refugees.
